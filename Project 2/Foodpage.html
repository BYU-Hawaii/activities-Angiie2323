Laplap is a traditional dish from Vanuatu made with grated root vegetables, coconut milk, and often meat or fish. It's wrapped in banana leaves and cooked over hot stones, resulting in a delicious and hearty meal. This dish is a symbol of community and tradition, often prepared and shared during special occasions. Laplap's rich flavors and cultural significance make it a must-try experience for visitors to Vanuatu.

Simboro is a traditional dish from Vanuatu that showcases the island's use of locally sourced ingredients and culinary techniques. This dish is an excellent example of how Vanuatu cuisine combines simplicity with rich, natural flavors. Simboro is typically served hot, often accompanied by a sauce made from additional coconut milk. It can be enjoyed as a main dish or a side dish, depending on the meal. The texture of the cooked root vegetables or bananas combined with the creamy coconut milk creates a comforting and satisfying dish.
